S5 CALD Workshops and Training

Phase 5 shifts towards an activation of neurodiversity-affirming practices across school and home environments to support all school-aged autistic students, rather than focusing solely on individual student plans. For families, this approach also supports parents and carers with more than one autistic young person and broadens the conversation across multiple contexts.

Why the shift? Digital resources support better accessibility for all participants, allow ongoing access after the workshop, and reduce environmental impact. Hands-on physical resources remain where they add most value — during the workshop itself.
A Key Change

Digital Workbooks Replace Print

Printed participant workbooks will be discontinued. Instead, participants will access high-quality digital resources, supporting:

  • Better accessibility and ongoing access to materials
  • Environmental sustainability

Participants can use their own devices (phone / tablet / laptop) to access these resources, but the focus will not be on referring to these actively across the sessions: if paper-based resources are required, they will be part of the kit resources, including laminated Diversity Wheels, Story Circles, Planning Tools and My Child's Storyboards.

This means that the majority, if not all, resources will not need to be printed and shipped to workshops; kit resources will be the resources used at workshops, in conjunction with participants using their own devices to access the digital resources shared with them before and after the workshops.

Digital Accessed on own devices

  • Summaries of key concepts
  • Curated videos and tools accessed throughout the workshops
  • Links to ongoing learning available on the LMS

Physical Kit Used at workshops

  • Laminated Diversity Wheels
  • Story Circles
  • Planning Tools
  • My Child's Storyboards
The shift in practice: Digital resources support better accessibility for all participants, allow ongoing access after the workshop, and reduce environmental impact. Hands-on physical kit resources remain where they add most value — during the workshop itself.

Delivery Model

Whole School Delivery

A three-visit model that maximises travel value and brings Positive Partnerships to both the school community and surrounding families.

Initial Visit

Whole School Workshop

Full-day professional learning workshop for the school's educators and staff.

Return Visit · Day 1

School Families Workshop

Parent and carer workshop specifically for families at the school.

Return Visit · Day 2

Community Workshop

Parent and carer workshop open to the wider local community.

Why this model? By returning to the same location, the team maximises travel value and reduces the need for separate standalone parent workshops in other locations — while ensuring both the school community and wider area benefit.

Positive Partnerships is funded by the Australian Government Department of Education.
